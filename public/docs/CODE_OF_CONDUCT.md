# Savannah Implementation Code of Conduct

## Purpose
This Code of Conduct outlines the standards of professional behavior and technical excellence expected from everyone involved in the implementation, maintenance, and expansion of the Savannah platform.

## 1. Professional Behavior
- **Integrity:** Be honest and transparent about progress, obstacles, and errors.
- **Respect:** Treat all team members—from developers to waitstaff—with professional courtesy.
- **Accountability:** Take ownership of your assigned tasks and their impact on the overall system.

## 2. Technical Standards
- **Excellence:** Strive for high-quality code that is well-documented, tested, and maintainable.
- **Security First:** Never compromise on data security. Protect customer and staff information at all costs.
- **Performance:** Optimize for speed and reliability, ensuring a luxury experience for the end-user.
- **Consistency:** Adhere to established design patterns and UI/UX guidelines (e.g., Glassmorphism).

## 3. Collaboration & Communication
- **Proactive Updates:** Communicate early if a deadline is at risk or if a technical blocker is found.
- **Constructive Feedback:** Provide and receive code reviews and design feedback with a focus on improvement.
- **Knowledge Sharing:** Document custom logic and architectural decisions to ensure the team can support your work.

## 4. Operational Integrity
- **Minimal Disruption:** Plan system updates and migrations to minimize downtime for the business.
- **Audit Trails:** Ensure critical actions are logged appropriately for transparency.
- **Compliance:** Adhere to local labor laws and industry regulations (GDPR, PCI-DSS) in all implementations.

## 5. Violations
Breaches of this Code of Conduct should be reported to the Project Lead or System Administrator. We prioritize a safe, professional, and productive environment for all Savannah contributors.
